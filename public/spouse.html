Marriage-Based Visas

US citizens and permanent residents may sponsor their spouses and children to live in the United States.

Key Facts:
  • Most visas permit the applicant to apply for visas for a spouse and unmarried children under 21 to join or accompany him or her.
  • Only permanent resident visas and certain other visas permit spouses to work in the United States.
  • The sponsor must typically demonstrate financial support for the spouse and children before a visa can be issued.
  • The time you have to wait for the visa depends on the sponsor's status in the United States (citizen, permanent resident, or other visa) and the applicant's country of origin.
  • Your attorney may file multiple applications at the same time to get you into the US as quickly as possible.
  • A sponsor and fiancé(e) ordinarily must have met in person in the last two years.
