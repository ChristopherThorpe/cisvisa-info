Where can I find a company to sponsor me?

This is a common question, and it's important – you generally need to have a job offer from a qualified employer before you can apply for a US work visa. Then you and your employer, together with an attorney, work through the visa process. We can't help you find a job, and we can’t give advice on specific firms. We can, however, explain your options and how to research them.

Your options depend on your education and skills

In general, the type of work visa you can get depends on your education. There are lots of exceptions to this, but if you treat 4 years of work experience like 1 year of university, here is a simple way of understanding it:

Education Types of work Possible visa Where to learn more
University graduate
(4+ years) 		Professional work H1-B Several major internet job sites have areas where H1-B employers and workers can find each other.
University professor J: scholar, specialist There are three programs for university-level scholars: existing sponsors are here, here, and here.
School teacher J: teacher Primary and secondary school positions. Many schools are already certified to sponsor.
University student
(or very recent graduate) 		Intern or trainee J: intern, trainee Work in a sponsoring US company as a current student or recent graduate.
Summer job (and tourism) J: summer work travel Students from some countries don’t need a pre-arranged job. Others need sponsors.
No university Au Pair J: Au pair Provide child care to a host family, usually through an Au Pair agency.
Summer Camp J: Camp counselor Work with children at a summer camp.
Farm Work H2-A These are sponsored by farms and agricultural companies, and change each season.
Seasonal: parks, hotels, resorts H2-B These tend to be resorts and tourist sites which need either summer (beach) or winter (skiing) help.
