Where can I find a school to attend?

In order to study in the USA, you need to attend a program that’s been certified to sponsor foreign students. The visa process starts after you have been accepted to study at a certified program, so a typical applicant will find a program first, then work with the program to get their visa. Schools which are used to dealing with foreign students will handle these routinely, but those who are less familiar might need an attorney’s help.

List of approved programs

There is an online list of the academic programs which have been approved to sponsor foreign students. It's possible to get a new program onto this list, but if yours is already there is makes the process easier.

More about the process

Good news – the US Government has built a useful web site called EducationUSA describing the student visa process and listing eligible programs.

It covers the major categories of study visas, which are generally F and J visas. These include many different types of education: language courses, secondary schools, short-term programs, colleges, universities, graduate degrees, and professional programs such as medicine.

During the process, you'll meet SEVIS.

SEVIS is the computer system the USA uses to track all of its foreign students and exchange program participants. It allows programs and students to do most common administrative tasks online. You can read more about it here.
