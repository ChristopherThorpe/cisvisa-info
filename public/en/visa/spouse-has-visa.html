Spouse and children
of visa holders

People who are legally in the USA, but who are not citizens are permanent residents, can often get permission for their spouses and children to join them while they are here.

Key Facts:


  • The primary visa holder, spouse and unmarried children under 21 (often called "derivatives") must all enter on the same visa type.
  • You and your spouse may apply for different visa types on your own. If you and your spouse have different visa types, your children may enter with you as derivatives of either visa.
  • For example, if your spouse is entering the United States on an H1-B visa, you may not work in the U.S. as a derivative. However, if your employer is willing to transfer you to their US office, they may apply for an L-1 visa so you can work here.
  • Each visa has different rules.
  • Under most visas, a spouse may not work while in the US. Common exceptions are the E‑1 (treaty trader), E‑2 (treaty investor), J‑2 (Exchange Visitor), L‑1 (Intracompany Transferee), and government (A, G and N) visas. To learn more, see Form I-765 Instructions.
  • Each of these visas has different rights, requirements, and application processes.
