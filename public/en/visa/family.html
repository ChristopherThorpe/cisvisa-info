Family-Based Visas

US citizens may sponsor children, parents, brothers, and sisters to immigrate to the USA. US permanent residents may sponsor children.

Learn about visas for spouses...

Key Facts:
  • Only spouses, children, parents and siblings are eligible for family-based visas. Uncles, aunts, cousins, and other relatives are not.
  • The sponsor ordinarily must agree to provide financial support to the applicant before a visa can be issued. Learn more about the Affidavit of Support.
  • Sponsors who are not citizens or permanent residents may ordinarily sponsor children (and spouses) to accompany or join them during their stay in the USA.
  • Only a limited number of visas permit spouses or family members to work while in the USA. An attorney can help you find the best option for your family's needs.
  • The waiting period for family preference "green cards" can be many years. Visit Nolo.com to learn more about waiting times.
  • Applying for a family preference "green card" may be interpreted as an intent to immigrate. This may mean that an applicant will not be able to visit the United States on most temporary visas during the waiting period.
