Adoption

"Each year thousands of U.S. citizens adopt children from abroad and many families in other countries adopt U.S. children. Intercountry adoption is governed by both the laws of the country in which the child lives and the country in which the adoptive parents live."
    — U.S. Dept. of State, at adoption.state.gov

Key Facts

  • If you are adopting a child from another country through an adoption agency, your agency will typically help you with the immigration process.
  • If you are adopting children born outside the United States without the assistance of an agency, the rules can be complex.
  • If you or your spouse is a U.S. citizen, the process is simple if handled properly. The process is more complex for permanent residents and legal visitors.
  • Whether you would like professional preparation or just a second pair of eyes, an attorney can help.
  • It is important to obtain citizenship for adopted children. According to adoption.state.gov, "If you postpone documenting or obtaining your child's citizenship, later he or she may have difficulty... In some cases, the child might even be subject to deportation." For this reason we recommend against handling adoption matters by yourself.
