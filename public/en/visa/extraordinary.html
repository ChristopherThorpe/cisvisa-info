Extraordinary abilities

These visas are for prominent or highly-skilled people. They cover artists, athletes, entertainers, fashion models, and some businesspeople.

Examples

  • Scientist with advanced degree, published research, and awards
  • Sports team attending an elite-level competition
  • Well-known movie or TV show actor
  • Technical expert who earns world-class fees for their professional work

Key Facts

  • You must be unusually good at what you do and be able to prove it. You should be winning awards, being mentioned in the press, or be internationally-known within your field.
  • Some of these visas are tied to a specific event, such as a concert. They allow you to bring others who are essential to the act, such as a band. Other types of these visas are for taking a job in the USA, with an organization that's ready to sponsor you. These can lead to a green card, and they allow you to bring family members.
  • Most of these visas (O, P) require you to have your event or job pre-arranged, but a few others don't (NIW, EB-2). The ones where you can avoid pre-arranging a job, however, have a high standard for achievement. They're harder to qualify for.
