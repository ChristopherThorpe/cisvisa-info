Study in the USA

Congratulations! You've been admitted to an approved program. This is usually the hardest part of securing your visa. Let's look at how this visa works and how to pursue the next steps of the process.

Key Facts


  • The timing of this visa is complicated. Once your visa is issued, you have 120 days to register with your program. However, you can only enter the US within 30 days of registering. So you either have to register while you're still abroad, register quickly once you get here, or enter on another visa type.
  • If you are already in the United States, you must apply to "adjust your status" to a student visa before your studies begin.
  • You have very limited right to work in the USA on a student visa. After all, you're supposed to be here studying. See below for more details.
  • As part of the application process, you'll have to show evidence of sufficient funds to pursue your proposed course of study.
  • You must intend to depart the US when your studies are complete. Be prepared to answer questions about this if you speak with a consular officer.
