Visit the USA

Visitor visas are granted for a specific, limited period for business, pleasure, or medical treatment. These are typically easier to obtain, but have important restrictions.
⇒ I intend to work in the USA...

Key Facts

  • Visitors from certain countries may be eligible to visit the USA for up to 90 days without a visa under the Visa Waiver Program, though some business activities still require a visa.
    Learn about the Visa Waiver Program.
  • You may not work on a visitor visa. You may not study for credit or 18 hours or more per week.
  • You must show evidence of funds to cover your expenses while in the United States, as well as a residence and social and economic ties abroad.
  • You must not have ties in the US that make you likely to stay. For example, you may not use a visitor visa to get married or to purchase and run a business.

Some Allowed Activities

  • Tourism, travel, sightseeing, etc.
  • Participate in a sports event
  • Seek investment or places to do business
  • Attend conferences, meetings, or trade shows
  • Sell, install, or maintain products sold by a non-US company
