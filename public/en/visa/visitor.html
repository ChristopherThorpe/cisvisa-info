Visit the USA

Visitor visas grant you a limited period of time to come to the US for tourism, business meetings, or medical treatment.

Examples

  • Touring parts of the United States
  • Participating in a recreational sports event
  • Seeking investment or places to do business
  • Attending conferences, meetings, or trade shows
  • Selling, installing, or maintaining products sold by a non-US company

Key Facts

  • Visitors from many countries can visit the USA for up to 90 days without a visa.
You can't work or study in the USA on a visitor visa. This confuses people, because you are allowed to do several sorts of activity that sounds like working and studying.

  • You can go to meetings, for example, attend conferences, or even repair machinery.
  • The key point is that you can't take a US-based job, either permanently or temporarily.
  • You can continue collecting your foreign salary (paid to you back in your home country) while you're here, but you can't get paid in the USA for doing anything new.
  • You may not study for credit or for more than 18 hours per week.
Consular or border officials will want to know that you intend to leave after your trip, and that you can afford to do so.

  • You must show evidence of funds to cover your expenses while in the United States, as well as a residence and social and economic ties abroad.
  • You must not have ties in the US that make you likely to stay. For example, you may not use a visitor visa to get married or to purchase and run a business.
