Naturalization (Citizenship)

For Green Card Holders:
  • You must have lived continuously in the US as a green card holder for the past 5 years. You must have been inside the United States for 30 months of those 5 years.
    Exception: You also qualify if you are married to a US citizen and have been living with your spouse as a green card holder for the past 3 years, and have been inside the United States for 18 months of those 3 years.
  • You must be 18 or older.
  • You must be able to read, write and speak English and pass a test on U.S. history and government (civics). Exceptions may apply for persons over 50 or with certain disabilities.
  • Special rules apply for families of members of the US armed forces, including when the service member died in combat. An attorney can help you in this complex situation.
Other Facts:
  • Many children born outside the United States qualify for US citizenship if one or both biological or adoptive parents is a US citizen. An attorney can help you understand the rules that apply to you.
  • Special rules apply for members of the US armed forces and their families, even if the service member died in combat. An attorney can help you with these complex rules.
