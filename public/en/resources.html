Resources that can help you

There are a variety of web sites offering visa advice. It’s hard to tell which ones you can trust. Our advice is: do your research and know who you’re dealing with before buying anything.

Our favorite web sites

We’re proud of our web site, and we hope it helps you, but there are other good sites out there. Here are our favorites. They have the best quality free information, and are good places to start learning about the visa process and your options.

  • USCIS and the US Department of State are the official US government sources for rules and documents. These websites are the definitive reference, but it's a complicated place to start out.
  • Nolo is a well-regarded non-profit which has advice about the process and guides for filling out the actual forms. You have to pay for some of the guides.
  • Immihelp and VisaJourney offer a variety of useful tips and free guides. They're both nice, immigrant-friendly websites with detailed help and checklists.

Another free option: the Diversity Visa

You should also know about the Diversity Visa lottery. This is an annual event, held each October, where the US Government gives green cards to 50,000 lucky winners from eligible countries. It's free to apply: never pay anyone a fee to help you apply.
  • We offer a free service to help people with the DV lottery.
  • You can also go directly to the official website.
  • Beware of scams. People we have helped have forwarded us emails telling them they have won the DV lottery. These are fake. Only entrants with official confirmation numbers can win the DV lottery, and the only way to be certain is to check your confirmation number yourself on the official government website. Never send money by Western Union. Pay your fees directly at the consulate or embassy.
