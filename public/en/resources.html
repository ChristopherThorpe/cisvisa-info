Resources that can help you

There are a variety of web sites offering visa advice. It’s hard to tell which ones you can trust. Our advice is: do your research and know who you’re dealing with before buying anything. But if you'd like to go beyond the information we've compiled on this site, here are some others which are worth reading.

Our favorite web sites

These sites have the best quality free information, and are good places to start learning about the visa process and your options.

  • USCIS and the US Department of State are the official US government sources for rules and documents. These websites are the definitive reference, but it's a complicated place to start out.
  • Nolo is a well-regarded non-profit which has advice about the process and guides for filling out the actual forms. You have to pay for some of the guides.
  • Immihelp and VisaJourney offer a variety of useful tips and free guides. They're both nice, immigrant-friendly websites with detailed help and checklists.

Another free option: the Diversity Visa

You should also know about the Diversity Visa lottery. This is an annual event, held each October, where the US Government gives green cards to 50,000 lucky winners from eligible countries. It's free to apply: never pay anyone a fee to help you apply.

  • Several people offer services helping you apply. We think ours is the best! And it's free, just like the lottery was meant to be.
  • You can also go directly to the official website, which accepts applications directly when the window is open. This usually happens in the late fall, around October, and is announced well in advance.
The Diversity Lottery is a wonderful thing, but you should beware of scams. We've seen emails where people are told they've won, or asking them to send money via wire transfer. These are all fake. The only way to find out your result is to check the official government web site with your confirmation number. If you win, the US consulate or embassy will tell you how to pay your fees.
