Investor visas

Examples of Investor Visas:
  • A woman from Belgium opened a consulting practice in the United States. She invested substantial capital ($75,000) to purchase office equipment, lease an office, and operate her business. She received an E-2 visa.
  • A man from Nigeria invested $1,000,000 to build a new restaurant in the United States and hired 10 new full-time employees. He and his family were granted EB-5 "green cards".
  • A large oil company from the UK decided to build a major project in Texas. The director of the business unit and dozens of his essential employees qualified for E-2 visas.
Key facts:
  • The E-2 treaty investor visa is for people who invest a substantial amount of capital in a business they direct.
  • A company employing E‑2 investors may sponsor them for a green card.
  • You and your family can obtain EB-5 green cards if you invest between USD $500,000 and $1,000,000 in a US-based business that creates 10 new full-time jobs.
  • Applicants for the E-2 visa may bring family members and essential employees.
  • E-2 spouses may work in the USA.
  • To see if you are eligible for an E-2 visa, visit the official list of treaty countries. (Look for the "E-2" classification.)
