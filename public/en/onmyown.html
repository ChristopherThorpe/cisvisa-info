Should I apply on my own?

The visa process is complicated and expensive, but if you’re willing to work hard to figure it out, it’s possible to be successful on your own.

Although you may save money on attorney fees, you also risk getting a step wrong and being delayed or rejected. Moreover, many applicants find that a skilled attorney saved them money and time by finding a less expensive visa option or avoiding unnecessary foreign travel and resubmission fees.

We can’t give you personalized advice on which choice is right for you, but we can help you understand what is involved with the process. This will help you make a better decision.

1 Understand your options. Read about the visas in more detail to make sure the one you apply for gives you (and your family) the rights you need to work, study, or travel. They’re all different.  
2 Plan your process. Each visa has specific steps and requirements. You’ll want to study them and make sure you know what to do, and when. Errors can delay the process, result in a rejected application, or even prevent you from entering the USA in the future.
3 Prepare and submit your application. You'll need to download and print a number of official forms. Many forms require additional documents proving things such as where you were born and how much education you’ve had, so you'll need to obtain certified copies of those documents. Documents not in English must be professionally translated. Always keep a complete copy of your entire submission.  
4 Pay the fees. Most visa applications require payment of fees. Even if you don't hire an attorney, you are responsible for processing and consular fees, which can cost well over $1,000 for many families.  
5 Respond to official letters. The US government may send you a "Request for Evidence" (RFE) or other notices. If you don't respond correctly or on time, your application could be rejected. If you receive a "Notice of Intent to Deny" (NOID), we strongly recommend you seek an attorney's assistance.    
6 Manage your appointments. Once your application is approved, you may be required to attend in-person appointments or interviews, such as a medical exam or a visit to the US consulate in your home country. You'll have to schedule and attend these to obtain your visa. Some visas require additional fees at this time, for example, for sending documents to the US.
7 Arrange travel to the USA. After receiving your visa, arrange your travel to the United States and complete your journey! ✈
